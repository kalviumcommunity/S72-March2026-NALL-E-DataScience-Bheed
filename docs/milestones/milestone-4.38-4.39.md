# Milestone 4.38 & 4.39 — Cross-column Analysis and Distribution Plots

In this final milestone of the sprint, we dive deeper into Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) and start visualizing our findings.

## Key Concepts

### 1. Cross-column Analysis
We explore the relationship between different variables. For example:
*   **Temperature vs. Visitors**: Does hotter weather in Jaipur lead to fewer tourists?
*   **Is Holiday vs. Visitors**: How much does a public holiday spike historical footfall?
*   **City-wise Aggregations**: Comparing total visitors across different cities to identify the busiest tourist hubs.

### 2. Distribution Plots
Visualizing the data helps us see patterns that numbers alone might hide.
*   **Histograms**: To see the frequency distribution of visitor counts.
*   **Bar Charts**: For comparing categorical data like average visitors per city.
*   **Line Plots**: For observing trends over time.

### 3. Dashboard Polish (Preview)
We begin thinking about how these static plots will translate into the interactive Streamlit dashboard. We refine our visualization labels, colors, and titles to ensure they are stakeholder-ready.

## Why this is the "final push" for BheedRadar?
By the end of this milestone, we have a clear understanding of the drivers of tourist footfall. These insights will directly feed into our forecasting models (Random Forest) in the next phase.
